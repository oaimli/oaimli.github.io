Research Interests

Miao's research focuses on natural language processing (NLP) and artificial intelligence (AI). During his PhD, he has primarily worked on multi-document summarisation, emphasising information integration and decompositional reasoning with large language models (LLMs) to enhance transparency and grounding in the automatic summarisation of multiple documents. His overarching research goal is to advance AI systems for integrative reasoning across multiple information sources, thereby transcending human brain limitations of processing speed and working memory, and facilitating more efficient, profound and scalable human understanding through human-AI collaboration. Specifically, his research interests include: (1) deepening our understanding of how humans cognitively correlate and integrate dispersed information for decision-making, e.g., making well-informed treatment decisions in healthcare, (2) developing computational models, with a current emphasis on long-context language models, that can reason over voluminous, heterogeneous, and distributed information at superhuman speeds, e.g., performing in-context question answering across hundreds of scientific articles, and (3) establishing controlled experimentation and high-quality evaluation for both model outputs and their inner workings and behaviours, ensuring these models are not only useful but also verifiable and explainable for real-world utility.
